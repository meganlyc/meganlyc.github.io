Proposal for July’s Unit 3 Exhibition

For the Unit 3 exhibition in July, I intend to present a piece titled “Looming Pillar,” constructed using tea bricks. This project explores the idea of looming presence and the sense of oppression that certain structures can impose. The tea bricks will form a towering, slightly leaning column that evokes a feeling of impending pressure and unease.

The pillar will stand as a powerful symbol of cultural and environmental weight, using the dense, dark texture of compressed tea to convey a sense of gravity and historical significance. The choice of tea bricks, a material with deep cultural roots, serves to underscore the themes of tradition and the burdens of the past that continue to influence the present. This project is deeply influenced by the principles of Mono-ha, emphasizing the interaction between the material and the space it occupies.

The installation will be accompanied by a series of photographs and a video documenting the creation process, providing viewers with insight into the meticulous stacking and balancing of the tea bricks. This documentation will highlight the nature of the work and the conceptual considerations behind the project.

Initial Proposal for Autumn’s Research Festival

For the autumn’s Research Festival, I propose a comprehensive written piece that delves into the conceptual and practical aspects of my work. This written piece will provide a critical reflection on the concept of “looming” as it pertains to art and architecture. It will analyze how physical structures can evoke emotional responses and the ways in which traditional materials can be used to convey contemporary concerns.

The essay will explore the influence of Mono-ha on my practice, particularly how the interaction between material and space creates a dialogue with the viewer. It will also discuss the cultural significance of using tea bricks, highlighting their historical and environmental implications. This piece will situate my work within broader artistic and cultural debates, offering a nuanced understanding of its significance.

By focusing on a detailed written analysis, I aim to create a rich and informative exploration of my work that deepens the audience’s appreciation of its thematic underpinnings. This aligns with the principles of Mono-ha, emphasizing the harmony between the material, its space, and the viewer's experience.
