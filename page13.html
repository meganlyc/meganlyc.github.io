1.The making of tea bricks:
First, fresh tea leaves are picked, rolled to release their aroma, and then sun-dried to reduce moisture. The leaves are then steamed, placed into molds, and compressed into shape. Finally, they undergo drying to form dense, easy-to-store tea bricks.

2.The making of tea bricks column
Creating the tea brick column involves stacking compressed tea bricks layer by layer, bonding each layer securely with no-nail adhesive. Each layer is carefully aligned to ensure the column’s stability and uniformity. Once stacking is complete, the adhesive is left to air dry, allowing the structure to solidify and achieve lasting support.

3. Structure
Next, a metal framework is crafted to the exact dimensions needed to support the tea brick column. This framework not only provides structural support but also ensures the stability of the sculpture. Once completed, the metal frame is carefully inserted into the interior of the tea brick column, fully embedding it to further enhance the sculpture’s stability.

4. The creation of the tea leaf surface (shadow).
Dried tea leaves are pounded and crushed into smaller pieces for easier spreading. A mixture of white glue and no-nail adhesive is prepared as a bonding agent, and the crushed tea leaves are evenly spread over the surface of the paper. Once complete, the arrangement is left to air dry, allowing the tea leaves to adhere securely to the paper, creating a stable, textured surface effect.
