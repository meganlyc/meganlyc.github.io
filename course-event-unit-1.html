Workshop with Rebecca Moss

Since I have done performance art projects before, Rebecca's workshop filled me with expectations. This performance art workshop was a brand new experience. During this process, we browsed many performance artists' works and appreciated rebecca's work. I am deeply impressed by rebecca's work. She combines humor with performance art and lightens serious topics through humorous performance art, so that people can enjoy art and feel fun at the same time. For me, this is a very unique way of expression, because most of the performance art I see is more like Yoko ono or Tehching Hsieh, which is more serious expression of works, so I find it very interesting.

Finally, we are asked to explore the relationship between the body, the surrounding objects and the surrounding environment within a limited time, and try to create performance art. In this process, I was thinking that perhaps performance art is to refine life into the essence of behavior, enlarge the daily life, and turn normal into "abnormal" and then express it.

In the process of this workshop, I also gradually understood some connotations of performance artists. First of all, the common feature of performance art is that it often takes human body or behavior as the basic material of creation. In performance art, the body is no longer the shell in the pure physiological sense, but the body infused by the artist's concept and reshaped by the artist, and the body becomes an expression of concept and artistic reconstruction. Secondly, performance art has strong anti-version and sociality. In addition, performance art has a public character, which has narrowed the relationship between art and the general public, and gradually eliminated the psychology that art is out of reach for ordinary people.

This workshop also made me start to think about the relationship between body, performance and sculpture, which I think can be an attempt for my future projects. I need to keep exploring how to better combine performance art and sculpture to express myself.

Language Development

Language Development courses are designed to help students master the English skills needed in their courses. Geraint asks us what problems we encounter in language in class. We summarize them. First of all, it is difficult for us to express some very professional words in English when describing the project. If so, it will be difficult for us to explain the project, and we need to clarify the logic when we explain the project. When we change to English, we may not be able to better explain the concept because of language problems. Secondly, we are always nervous during the presentation, which will cause us to breathe faster, speak too fast, and even forget the words, which is a bad experience for both the listener and the presenter. Finally, language will affect our daily communication and communication with teachers, and good English communication skills will be very convenient and happy when we encounter any problems or academic exchanges.

In a language development class, each of us was asked to explain our works without making any mistakes. Giranit put some common sentence patterns on the screen to help us better transition the sentences to the next time in the narration and better help us express the works. Under such training, I gradually learned to adjust my breathing, slow down my speaking speed, constantly give myself confidence and then slowly express myself, which I think is a great progress.

Reading Group

Mika Hannula -The Politics of Small Gestures
I have mainly read the introduction on pages 6-12. Although it is only an introduction, it impressed me deeply. The core meaning of this part is to explain that we should not be disturbed by many things, and we should not think of making quick money in art. Don't make your contemporary art too commoditized, don't be nationalistic, racist, make it pure art that people all over the world can perceive.

The content mentioned in this book has really triggered my thinking about the commercialization of contemporary art. Some people think that artists create works of art through their labor, and once they enter the market, they become art commodities. Art is originally priceless. When art works enter the market as commodities, it will be a kind of market to measure art with value. Therefore, the market will undoubtedly become the main form and means to realize and embody the value of art works. However, art is very special, and it is different from general commodities because its use value is mainly to meet people's spiritual needs. Its value is also different from that of ordinary mental or manual labor, and it also contains non-human factors such as "talent". In addition, the artistic level, the popularity of the author and so on also affect the value of art commodities, so the value of art commodities is very complicated.

Art also belongs to social ideology and is the superstructure of society. Economic prosperity will inevitably lead to art prosperity, and art prosperity will inevitably promote economic prosperity. Simple thinking process and abstract concept can only exist in the human brain, can not be communicated, can not be appreciated by others, can not meet the purpose of social spiritual aesthetic needs. Without a certain material basis, any spiritual production, including artistic production, is impossible, so in the view of many people, money is the material basis for the development of art, and many artists are also bent over.

However, I am also thinking that commercialization does guarantee the material life of artists to some extent, but it may also affect their ability to create better in the tide of economy. In addition, after the commercialization of art is measured by money, people may be more concerned about the economic value of artworks and ignore its own aesthetics, history, spiritual value, etc., then how should art develop? With these questions in mind I intend to continue reading the book.

Finally, I would like to say that the weekly reading group not only allows me to read a lot of books that I have never read before, but also I enjoy the collision and communication of ideas with you, which is an opportunity for me to improve myself, and I really enjoy such discussion time.
