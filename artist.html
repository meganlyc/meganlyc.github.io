My work is a combination of abstract sculpture and smoke, trying to think about the connection between simulacrum and the subconscious, and discussing a kind of authenticity by thinking about the point that the subconscious controls people.

The theme of the work starts from thinking about the connection between simulacrum and dreams. When a person is dreaming, the brain will convert simple imagery thinking in the brain into image output in the dream state but cannot call complex brain thinking instructions, while the transmitting and receiving nerves are also in a largely cut-off state and cannot call the entire stored time-space images, much less the true or false recognition of images. Therefore, what happens in dreams, i.e. all the imaginary pictures and images in people's minds at a particular moment when they close their eyes is similar to a kind of organic simulacrum in the human brain。 However, the organic simulacrum is somehow deceptive. Therefore, killing dreams and killing organic simulacrum (i.e., killing the deception by dreams) becomes a process of pursuing the real - infinitely closer to the real.

In this case, I used clay as the initial material. Clay has a very unique nature: it has tension and perception. When people apply force to the clay, it’s like a mutual perception between people and nature, creating a dialogue between physical objects and mental images. I “create” things through understanding of nature and my perception at the moment, giving form to my imagination. Then I used 3d technology to enlarge the clay sculpture, but still retain the texture of the clay surface of the sculpture.

Finally, I added smoke to the work, using smoke as a reflection of the continuous vitality of the dream. Smoke is produced through the sculpture, and whether the continuous smoke will eventually tend to die out and get a kind of reality but also give people the space to question and imagine.

Through this work, I keep thinking that I am the creator of my dreams, yet I try to kill the deception of dreams. It seems contradictory but reflects my (i.e., human's) fear of losing autonomy in a way. The smoke comes from ceramic ware. It is generated and dissipated in a cycle, indicating that human wants to break free but cannot escape from the control of the simulacrum image brought by the dreams (subconscious mind/thoughts) that are constantly generated, dissipated, and generated again. However, people are afraid of the simulacrum of being manipulated, but the crueler truth may be that the manipulator is often ourselves. That is, we ourselves are creating the illusion and constantly controlling ourselves, which is called “contradiction”. We are afraid of losing our autonomy, but we are creating this feeling in our own subconscious. “Creating this feeling” is implying to us in another way that we should not lose it but grasp it. I try to reflect on such a state of mind.
