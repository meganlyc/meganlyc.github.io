LAYER OF ILLUSION

My work crafted from a blend of plaster, ceramics, paper pulp, fabric, and tulle, try to transcends mere physical forms. It serves not only as a piece of art but also can be seen as an intriguing representation of a cat and a functional lamp. Embracing the Bauhaus ethos of form and function, it features bold colors, geometric shapes, and clean lines that challenge and redefine the aesthetics and utility of everyday objects.

Incorporating contemporary totem design, the sculpture layers various materials to create a vertical composition that echoes traditional totems, with each layer bearing distinct significance. This exploration of scale invites viewers to contemplate how a small object can captivate attention and alter the perception of a larger space. The integration of masks within its layers deepens this dialogue, introducing themes of identity and transformation common in totemic art. In many cultures, masks act as a bridge to the spiritual realm, encouraging people to reevaluate the roles and functions of everyday objects.

Utilizing digital technology, I experiment with displaying my sculptures in diverse contexts to observe how these settings alter the work’s meaning. For instance, I have photoshopped the sculpture into a furniture store image, making it appear as if it were a store item, or presented it in an online marketplace as if it were a designer piece. Through these processes, I explore the boundaries between art, furniture design, and consumer goods. These experiments aim to prompt the audience to reflect on and understand the different meanings and values of art and everyday life objects.

Therefore, this piece not only merges function with fantasy but also uses its scale to influence and redefine the space it occupies. It challenges us to see life within the lifeless, to recognize the beauty of utility in small forms, and to foster a deeper engagement with our everyday environment. Ultimately, it stimulates our contemplation of the dynamic interplay between art, utility, and space.
