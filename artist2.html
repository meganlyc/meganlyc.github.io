Crafted from a blend of plaster, ceramic, papier-mâché, fabric, and tulle, my artwork transcends mere physical form to function not only as a piece of art but also as a playful cat representation and a practical lamp. Embracing the Bauhaus ethos of merging form with function, it features bold colors, geometric shapes, and clean lines that challenge and redefine both the aesthetic and utility of everyday objects.

Incorporating a contemporary totem design, the sculpture layers various materials to create a vertical assemblage that echoes totemic traditions, where each tier holds distinct significance. This exploration of scale invites viewers to consider how such a small object can command significant attention and transform the perception of larger spaces. The addition of masks within its tiers further enriches this dialogue, introducing themes of identity and transformation—common in totemic art. Masks serve as connectors to spiritual realms in many cultures, here prompting a reevaluation of the persona and function embedded in everyday objects.

As this sculpture stands in a furniture store as a lamp, it becomes a metaphorical piece of furniture, urging a reconsideration of the roles of art and utility in our daily environments. It asks viewers to look beyond conventional uses, exploring the symbolic and transformative potential of functional objects.

Thus, this piece not only merges the functional with the fantastical but also leverages its scale to impact and redefine the space it inhabits. It challenges us to see the animate in the inanimate and recognize the utilitarian beauty in small forms, encouraging a deeper engagement with our everyday surroundings and provoking thoughts on the dynamic interplay between art, utility, and space.
